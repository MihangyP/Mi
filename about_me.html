Hello, my name is Mihangy Pierrot. I’m currently studying Computer Science at 42 Antananarivo. I don’t have a particular specialization yet—I’m exploring a bit of everything. If I were to describe myself, I'd simply say I’m a programmer, meaning someone who creates computer programs, no matter the type.

My passion for this field began back in high school, in my sophomore year, when my dad bought our first desktop computer. Naturally curious, I wanted to display a black screen with green text like in the movies. With the help of YouTube tutorials, I managed to do it, and I was hooked, even though I barely understood what I was doing at first.

After high school, I couldn’t immediately pursue computer science due to financial constraints. Instead, I went into a public university, studying Economics. In my second year, I took a web development course through SAYNA, funded by the International Organization of La Francophonie. There, I learned to create websites using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Node, and SQL.

After that course, I trained myself in React and React Native. About a year later, I decided to leave Economics and joined 42 Antananarivo. This is where I truly learned to program.

If I had to name the programming language I’m most proficient in, I’d say there’s just one for now: C.
